What is AHV?

Aggregate Human Velocity is the sum, over every moving human, of (people × speed) — the rate at which humanity as a whole covers distance. Built from the flow lens: annual passenger-kilometres by mode ÷ hours per year. The median human right now is doing 0 mph; the average, once you count all the stationary time, is under half a mile per hour.

Deep Dive — North American Consumer Auto

Non-commercial, non-government miles driven by the public in the US, Canada & Mexico, split by vehicle type. A subset of the global Road mode.

Run 2 · Deep Dive

North Americans personally drive ~2.85 trillion vehicle-miles a year — ~4.51 trillion passenger-miles, an AHV of ~515 million person-mph. That one slice of life nearly equals the aggregate velocity of all commercial aviation on Earth.
