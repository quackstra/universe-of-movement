# Off-Earth — Aggregate Human Velocity Analysis

> Part of the [Universe of Movement](../../../README.md) project. Run 2.

## Executive Summary

At any given moment, ~**10 humans** are off the planet — roughly 7 aboard the ISS
and 3 aboard Tiangong — each travelling at ~**17,150 mph** (orbital velocity,
~7.66 km/s, [NASA](https://www.nasa.gov/international-space-station/)). They are,
individually, **the fastest-moving humans alive** — ~30× faster than a cruising
airliner. Yet because there are only ~10 of them, their contribution to Aggregate
Human Velocity is ~**0.17 million person-mph** — about **0.004%** of the Big
Number. The ultimate speed-vs-headcount extreme, and the mirror image of active
travel (millions of people, walking pace).

## Key Findings

1. **The fastest humans alive**: an ISS astronaut laps the Earth every ~92
   minutes — the single highest instantaneous human speed in the ground frame.
2. **Negligible to the Big Number**: 10 people, however fast, can't move the
   aggregate — the inverse of the "ubiquity beats speed" finding for walking.
3. **The one mode where the High-Mobility scenario is transformative**: commercial
   LEO stations + orbital tourism could grow off-Earth AHV 25× by 2050 — still a
   rounding error, but the fastest-growing mode in percentage terms.

## Data Quality & Limitations
- Crew counts fluctuate with rotations/dockings; ~10 is a typical average.
- Suborbital tourism (Blue Origin/Virgin Galactic) is episodic and tiny; folded
  in at 5% share, 🔴.
